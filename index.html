His way

I don't come here to teach.
I come to share — the golden keys,
the understanding, the love,
and this moment — here, now —
where everything simply is.

"To be a teacher is easy.
To be a true friend is not.

What this path gives

Three things I wish
for every person
on this walk

I
To remember
to love yourself

Not as a practice. Not as an affirmation. As a rediscovery of what was never lost — only forgotten.

II
To fall in love
with meditation

Not as discipline or technique. As the natural return to the source — the place from where you never truly left.

III
To live
without fear

All of it. The daily anxieties. The existential dread. The fear of death itself. Dissolved — here, now — in presence.

▸ Design annotations — for discussion
Hero section
Left panel: Full-height portrait photo of Prashantam with his own quote overlaid. Right panel: Name, Zen proverb, and two CTAs — retreats (primary) and YouTube (secondary). The split communicates: presence on the left, invitation on the right.
Navigation
Five items only: About, Golden Keys, Retreats, School, and "Join the Walk" as the primary CTA — echoing the channel description language. No clutter. Every item earns its place.
Philosophy band
Dark section immediately below the hero — establishes his philosophy before anything else. The Burmese saying lives here, not buried in an About page. This is the soul of the site, visible within the first scroll.
Three gifts
Self-love, meditation, living without fear — his three deepest wishes for every visitor. Not listed as "what you'll get." Offered as what he wishes for you. The framing matters enormously.
YouTube integration
The website previews the four content types (Golden Key, Story, Guided Meditation, Kyotaku) so new visitors understand the channel before clicking through. Reduces friction, increases conversion to subscribers.
Email capture
"The Inner Letter" — named, purposeful, with a free guided meditation as the offer. The note beneath the form: "No noise. No selling. Only what belongs on the path." This is the owned audience that survives any platform change.
